Meeting Title: LLM Selection for Memora
Participants: Sneha, Darshan, Vivek
Date: 10 April

---

Vivek: Okay guys, we need to finalize the LLM today. This is critical for our system.

Sneha: Yeah, I think we should go with OpenAI. It’s proven, stable, and gives very high-quality outputs.

Darshan: True, but cost is a big issue. We’re going to have multiple queries, embeddings, responses… it’ll scale quickly.

Vivek: That’s my concern too. We don’t want to hit API limits during the demo.

Sneha: Fair, but quality matters. If answers are bad, the whole system fails.

Darshan: I’ve been testing Gemini recently. It’s actually quite fast and the free tier is pretty generous.

Sneha: Gemini? Is it reliable enough for reasoning tasks?

Darshan: For our use case — yes. We’re doing RAG, so the model is not generating blindly. It’s grounded on retrieved data.

Vivek: That’s important. Since we’re using retrieval, we don’t need the absolute best generative model.

Sneha: That makes sense. But what about OpenAI vs Gemini performance?

Darshan: OpenAI is slightly better in complex reasoning, but Gemini is faster and cheaper. For MVP, Gemini is a better tradeoff.

Vivek: What about local models? Like using Ollama?

Sneha: That would solve privacy concerns.

Darshan: Yeah but setup is heavy, and performance isn’t great on our systems. Also, we don’t have time to optimize that.

Vivek: Agreed. Let’s not risk it during hackathon.

Sneha: Okay, so we prioritize speed + cost over marginal quality gain?

Darshan: Exactly. And we can always switch later.

Vivek: Sounds good. So final decision?

Sneha: Yes, I’m okay with Gemini for now.

Darshan: Same.

Vivek: Done then.

---

Final Decision:
We will use Gemini as the primary LLM for Memora due to its speed, cost efficiency, and sufficient performance for RAG-based reasoning. OpenAI and local models may be considered in future iterations.
