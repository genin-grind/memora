**Meeting: Cloud Platform Finalization (AWS vs Azure)**
**Participants:** Sneha, Darshan, Vivek

---

**Sneha:** Thanks for joining. Today we need to finalize our cloud provider so we can move ahead with deployment. We’re choosing between AWS and Azure. Let’s weigh them quickly.

**Darshan:** From a technical standpoint, AWS gives us more flexibility with compute, storage, and AI services. Their ecosystem is mature, and there’s better support for agent-based architectures and scalable APIs.

**Vivek:** Azure is solid too, especially with enterprise integrations. But for our current needs—containerized services, serverless options, and cost control—AWS gives us more options and easier scalability.

**Sneha:** Cost-wise, AWS has more granular pricing models. Also, most open-source and third-party tools integrate more smoothly with AWS. That reduces engineering overhead.

**Darshan:** Another factor is speed. Our MVP needs fast iteration. AWS gives us better documentation, a larger community, and faster debugging support.

**Vivek:** Agreed. Azure would make sense if we were deeply tied into enterprise Microsoft tools. Right now, that’s not our situation.

**Sneha:** So we’re aligned? We proceed with AWS for this phase?

**Darshan:** Yes.

**Vivek:** Yes, AWS it is.

**Sneha:** Great. Decision finalized: we move forward with AWS. Next step is setting up infrastructure and deployment pipelines.
